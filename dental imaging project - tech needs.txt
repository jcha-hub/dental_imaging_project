Dental Imaging project - technical resources requests


What are you trying to accomplish?
	Pilot feasibility of using dental CBCT imaging data with the OHDSI Medical Imaging Extention.
		- Convert dental CBCT DICOM files (ToothFairy) into features in the OMOP image_occurrence table
        - Use pretrained segmentation model DentalSegmentator and tooth-level model ToothSeg on a second CBCT dataset MMDental (NIfTI format with medical records csv). 
		Limited annotations (20-30) will be done on 3D slicer on the local laptop for limited fine tuning of the ToothSeg model on the MMDental dataset. 
		AI extracted clinical features of missing teeth and impacted wisdom teeth will be used to populate the OMOP image_feature table.
		- Compare AI extracted features with clinical ground truth from medical records csv file from MMDental dataset
	Project impact - Proof of concept to determine if dental imaging can be standardized with modifications of the existing OHDSI medical imaging
	extension. Identify the gaps in OMOP dental terminiology and imaging vocabulary that need to be addressed to enable future multi-modal, multi-site research
	integrating dental imaging with EHR data.

i-CAT 3D Imaging System. The scans were performed in “Extended Field” mode, with a field of view (FOV) measuring
16 cm in diameter and 22 cm in height. The scanning process involved two scans of 20 seconds each, resulting in a voxel size of 0.4 mm.
